Structure of the organization

GitHub, Inc. was originally a flat organization with no middle managers, instead relying on self-management.[18] Employees could choose to work on projects that interested them (open allocation), but the chief executive set salaries.[19] In 2014, the company added a layer of middle management in response to serious harassment allegations against its senior leadership. As a result of the scandal, Tom Preston-Werner resigned from his position as CEO.[20]
